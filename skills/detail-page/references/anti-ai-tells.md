# Anti-AI tells — the mechanism, and the positive counter

The banned-defaults list in `aesthetics.md` is a list of *negatives*. This file is its missing other half: **why** a page still reads as AI even after it passes every negative check, and the **concrete positive move** that fixes each tell. Cross-sourced from 2024–2026 practitioner writing, trend press, and the homogenization literature (sources at the bottom).

> **This is not hypothetical.** It was written against a real case: a Korean live-webinar page (`01_webinar.html`) built with this skill, which **passed every mechanical, anti-slop, and taste gate** and was still called "AI" by a human reviewer. Its confirmed tells — from real screenshot tiles — were: mono-label garnish on every eyebrow/date/tag (IBM Plex Mono), an acid/kelly-green flood over black display type, a giant ghosted outline "10", identical browser/Notion window-chrome mockups, uniform outline chips, and every section built to the same rhythm (green header band → letter-square avatar → label → content). Every one of those is a numbered tell below.

## The meta-insight — read this first

Negative constraints and abstract meta-rules ("commit color", "be bold") do **not** move a model off the distribution — they relocate it to the **next-highest-probability mode**. By 2025 that second mode (mono eyebrows, acid-green-on-near-black, ghost numerals, browser-chrome mockups, outline chips) has itself been scraped and converged on: **"AI avoiding AI slop" is now its own recognizable slop genre.**

The deep mechanism: **every AI tell is content-agnostic decoration.** The monospace labels label nothing that needed labeling; the numerals count nothing sequential; the window chrome frames no real product; the grid varies for no editorial reason. Human taste is a chain of low-probability choices **each motivated by this specific content, audience, and culture** — a Korean webinar page should look like Korean commerce persuasion, not a Linear-clone dev tool.

The consequence for how you must work: **you cannot prompt your way to distinctiveness with adjectives, only with nouns.** "Bold" samples the statistical center of the word "bold." A *named* technique — `slab-serif price stamp`, `duotone reportage photograph`, `chiaroscuro portrait`, `vermilion single-accent` — collapses the sampling space to a point instead of the centroid of a cloud. That is what this skill's new `references/design-lexicon.md` + `scripts/keyword-picker.js` + `references/design-process.md` exist to force: **noun-space steering, chosen per section.**

## The tell list

Each tell: **why it reads AI**, the **positive counter** (a move, not just an avoidance), and the **keywords** to reach for (all live in `design-lexicon.md`).

**1. Monospace-label garnish** *(typography)* — mono eyebrows/kickers on every section. **Why AI:** the "technical credibility" costume copied from Vercel/Linear/Cursor; on non-technical content it reads as costume. The tell is density + indiscriminacy — a human uses one mono accent where data actually appears; AI monos every metadata row. **Counter:** reserve mono strictly for genuinely tabular strings (times, prices, file sizes); set section markers in the display face itself or with a real editorial device. **Keywords:** `small caps (real)` · `kicker in the text face` · `hanja garnish (記錄 一)` · `rubrication` · `run-in head` · `caption discipline` · single mono accent, once.

**2. Acid/kelly-green flood + near-black display** *(color)* — the "safe-distinctive" palette. **Why AI:** after purple was banned, brat-green-on-near-black became the highest-probability *escape* — boldness with zero risk, already dated per 2026 fashion press. One hex, no palette logic, no undertone, no working shades. **Counter:** a 3–5 swatch palette with an off-key relationship, or shift the green somewhere unphotographed; specify by name/hex per section. **Keywords:** `tone-on-tone (hue-drift, no black)` · `jewel tones` · `earth/clay palette` · `warm paper ground` · `tinted near-black (먹색)` · `color gel lighting` (shoot the color as light, don't flood a div).

**3. Giant ghosted outline numeral as decoration** *(layout)* — a lone outlined "10" counting nothing, touching nothing. **Why AI:** the zero-commitment way to be huge. Humans stole big numerals from 90s magazines where they were cropped, inked solid, overlapped, tied to a real list. **Counter:** make the numeral earn its size, or replace it with a big cropped word from the content. **Keywords:** `type as container` (the glyph carries real cargo) · `cropped display numeral / bleed crop` · `CMYK registration misprint` · `masthead-scale word (라이브)`.

**4. Fake browser/window-chrome mockups repeated as the only imagery** *(imagery)* — three traffic-light dots, repeated identically. **Why AI:** the visual lorem ipsum — "nothing real to show" — importing dev-tool semantics into non-software content. **Counter:** real captures of the real thing, or physical artifacts photographed. **Keywords:** `in-situ mockup` (design composited into a real photographed device) · `angled device photography` · `annotated screenshot (real marker circles)` · `reportage photography` · `insert shot`.

**5. Uniform thin-outline chips/tags as the only container device** *(layout)* — 1px pill chips on every tag. **Why AI:** the shadcn/Tailwind badge default; identical hairline boxes expose the single-primitive loop. **Counter:** delete 80% of chips; survivors get ONE treatment tied to meaning. **Keywords:** `solid-fill lozenge` · `tinted chip hierarchy` · `typeset metadata line (date · place · price)` · `catalog plate layout` · `poster credit block`. **3rd-generation survival:** the same primitive loop can reincarnate as document frames — see #22.

**6. Structural monotony — identical section rhythm all the way down** *(structure)* — the tell that most defined the webinar case. **Why AI:** sequential generation samples each section from the same conditional distribution; nothing escalates, breathes, or surprises. Human editorial design plans a **pacing arc**, which requires global intent. **Counter:** score the page's volumes before layout — whisper/speak/SHOUT; alternate full-bleed vs contained; break the grid 1–2× at peaks; one section 3× taller, one a single line. **Keywords:** `section rhythm / pacing` · `jump cut rhythm` · `full-bleed interruption` · `one-line palate-cleanser section` · `asymmetric split (70/30)` · `editorial spread logic`.

**7. Texture-less flat vector — zero grain, photography, or material depth** *(imagery)* — everything born in CSS/SVG is mathematically flat, and by 2026 flawless flatness itself signals machine origin. **Counter:** one committed material system — or simply use photography; one real photo destroys more AI-smell than any layout fix. **Keywords:** `film grain (3–6%)` · `halftone` · `risograph` · `paper-texture ground` · `duotone photography` · `shallow DOF / bokeh` · `texture close-up`.

**8. Dev-tool cosplay — Linear/Vercel language on non-dev content** *(meta)* — terminal typography on a Korean general-audience live event is a genre error the audience feels before naming it. **Counter:** choose the genre from the *content's* world. **Keywords:** `art direction` with a non-web referent (`전시 도록`, `신문 호외`, `필드노트`) · `concert-poster genre` · `29CM 기획전 editorial` · `broadcast lower-third` · `magazine feature-spread`.

**9. Letter-square placeholder avatars used as final design** *(imagery)* — single-letter colored squares are every framework's empty-state fallback; shipping them says "no real people were available." **Counter:** real portraits (even phone photos, treated consistently), silhouettes, or a real monogram/seal system. **Keywords:** `duotone portrait treatment` · `cut-out silhouette` · `monogram/seal (도장) system` · `end-credits sequence` (for a roster) · `Rembrandt/chiaroscuro portrait`.

**10. Numbered pseudo-editorial scaffolding (기록 01 · 첨부 02 · STEP 03)** *(structure)* — numbering as a coherence prosthetic; the numbers are inert (nothing refers back, nothing is navigable, items aren't sequential). The layout version of an em-dash tic. **Counter:** number only true sequences; elsewhere use named chapters, a TOC that actually links, or thematic titles. **Keywords:** `named chapters` · `real table of contents (anchored)` · `thematic section titles (현장 · 증명 · 조건)` · `true timeline device` · `hanja section markers`.

**11. Badge/eyebrow above H1 hero formula** *(layout)* — the tiny tracked label above an oversized centered headline, treated as mandatory furniture. **Counter:** fold the eyebrow's info into the headline; open with a dateline, a spoken-voice quote, or the strongest concrete fact as the headline. **Keywords:** `dateline opener` · `quote-hook opener` · `fact-first headline` · `magazine cover logic` · `no-eyebrow hero`.

**12. The overused AI font cluster (Inter/Space Grotesk/Geist/Instrument Serif/IBM Plex Mono)** *(typography)* — bundled tool defaults that saturate training data; the Korean version is Pretendard-for-everything with a Latin mono garnish. **Counter:** name the face per mood. **Keywords:** `격동고딕` · `격동명조` · `Chosun Ilbo Myungjo` · `리디바탕` · `Gmarket Sans` · `myeongjo display` · custom lettering for one hero word.

**13. All-caps/letter-spaced labels as the only hierarchy tool** *(typography)* — case + tracking are single CSS properties, so the model fakes hierarchy with them instead of committing to size/weight/color. **Counter:** a real modular scale (1.25–1.5 jumps), weight contrast (Thin vs Black), one emphasis color; tracking reserved for a single ceremonial element. **Keywords:** `modular scale 1.333` · `weight-contrast pairing (Thin/Black)` · `single emphasis color` · `optical sizing`.

**14. Bento-grid reflex** *(layout)* — post-Apple, bento is the model's answer to "organize features," but generated bentos have arbitrary cell sizes and equal visual temperature. **Counter:** use it only when cell size encodes importance; otherwise a different organizing metaphor per content type. **Keywords:** `importance-weighted cells` · `comparison table` · `timeline layout` · `editorial spread`.

**15. VibeCode purple / indigo-gradient palette** *(color)* — Tailwind's `indigo-500` default saturated training data; its presence is instant disqualification. **Counter:** the cure was never a different single hue — it's palette *logic*. **Keywords:** `named hex palette per section` · `analogous-plus-discord accent` · `palette from photograph`.

**16. Colored left-border accent strip on cards** *(layout)* — "almost as reliable a sign of AI design as em-dashes for text"; a shadcn-era one-utility-class default. **Counter:** differentiate cards by tint shift, whitespace grouping, or typographic label — edge OR elevation, never a decorative side-stripe. **Keywords:** `background tint shift` · `whitespace grouping` · `edge-or-elevation rule`.

**17. Glassmorphism, glow borders, gradient orbs** *(color)* — the v0/Cursor signature: decorative depth with no light source or material logic. **Counter:** one consistent light model, or fully flat printed-matter logic; delete glows that don't indicate interactive state. **Keywords:** `single light source rule` · `paper-layer collage depth` · `state-only glow`.

**18. Uniform fade-in-on-scroll and bounce easing everywhere** *(motion)* — motion applied globally as a polish pass; everything enters the same way at the same speed. **Counter:** choreograph 2–3 moments max, ease-out only, everything else static; motion marks the pacing arc's peaks. **Keywords:** `scroll choreography` · `staggered entrance (one list)` · `ease-out expo` · `motion budget (2–3 moments)`.

**19. Aphoristic AI copy cadence ("It's not X, it's Y", em-dash chains, buzzword verbs)** *(copy)* — copy converges on rhetorical templates the way layout converges on a skeleton; on Korean pages it shows up as translated-feeling 개조식 rhythm. **Counter:** the speaker's actual voice, concrete nouns and numbers, verbatim quotes (typos intact); read every headline aloud. **Keywords:** `verbatim testimonial` · `spoken-register headline` · `concrete noun+number rule` · single em-dash budget.

**20. Western-minimal register overriding local commerce vernacular** *(meta)* — a sparse Swiss-technical page reads foreign-made (= machine-made) to a Korean 상세페이지 audience whose genre is dense, photo-led, 후킹→공감→해결→증명→CTA with real price/deadline urgency. **Counter:** anchor to the local genre explicitly and keep its load-bearing organs. **Keywords:** `29CM PT format` · `와디즈 스토리텔링 구조` · `고밀도 증명 섹션` · `KakaoTalk capture proof` · `price/deadline urgency block`.

**21. Second-generation convergence — the "anti-slop" look is itself a mode** *(meta)* — the community's anti-purple advice (dark + mono + brutalist + acid accent) was published, scraped, and became the second attractor. Any un-anchored generation walk ends at an attractor. **Counter:** replace adjective-space steering with **noun-space steering** — per section, force selection from a concrete vocabulary; require one content-motivated "weird decision" per page; validate with *"which specific choice here could ONLY belong to this project?"* **Known attractor instances:** (1st) VibeCode purple; (2nd) dark+mono+brutalist+acid — the documented anti-purple mode; (3rd, observed in this skill's own output lineage) **archival document/ephemera pastiche** — a simulated paper world (uniform hairline document frames, typeset fake handwriting, smooth vector stamps, 기록/문서 numbering) with zero captured artifacts. **Guard rule:** the archival genre is permitted only when each section claiming it contains >=1 genuinely captured physical artifact (flatbed scan or photograph with real shadow/edge); if simulated-paper devices (vector stamp, typeset slip, CSS frame, fake tape) outnumber captured artifacts, flag convergence. **Keywords:** `noun-space steering` · `per-section vocabulary picker` · `one weird decision rule` · `named-reference anchoring` · `scanography` · `archival ephemera (real flatbed scan)`.

**22. Uniform document-frame loop** *(layout)* — identical 1–2px ink hairline rectangles repeat as the page's only container primitive. **Why AI:** this is the same single-primitive generation loop as #5 after outline chips were banned; the shape changed from chip to "document frame" but the mechanism survived. The paperwork genre becomes an alibi when the frames do not belong to real artifacts, do not encode content, and no alternative grouping device appears. **Detection cue:** count containers sharing the same border signature (width, color, radius). If one signature exceeds 4/page and non-border grouping devices are absent, it is a loop. **Cap rule:** identical border-signature containers <=4/page + at least 2 non-border grouping devices. A true frame is reserved for one real scanned document artifact with paper edge and real shadow. **Counter:** `background tint shift` · `whitespace grouping` · `cross-gutter overlap` · `catalog plate layout` · `paper-layer collage depth`.

**23. Typeset fake hand-annotation** *(imagery)* — a "handwritten" slip meant to prove human touch is actually a neat bordered box with serif type tilted a few degrees. **Why AI:** rotation is not handwriting. Repeated identical glyphs, a perfectly level baseline, and a vector border read as a word processor turned sideways; handwriting fonts fall into the same trap when their glyphs repeat mathematically. A fake-human proof mark becomes a tell amplifier because it makes the whole page less trustworthy. **Counter ladder:** (1) `hand-drawn annotation` as a real marker/pencil scan with wobbly line quality; (2) `brush calligraphy accent` as a one-off real stroke; (3) honest typeset `sidenote / marginalia` with 0deg rotation and no fake slip frame; (4) handwriting font only with many alternates, baseline drift, and no box. **Pixel acceptance:** stroke-width variation, baseline drift, and at least 3 different renderings of repeated letters; identical glyph repetition rejects it.

## How to use this in the loop

- In **planning** (SKILL.md steps 2–3 / `design-process.md` stages 2–3): for each tell, if your plan reaches for it, swap in the positive counter *by name*. The banned cluster is the anti-reference list on your Direction Card.
- In the **crit** (`design-process.md` stage 8 / `review-rubric.md`): this is the shift that catches the webinar failure. A rules-based lint checks the *absence* of banned defaults and passed the webinar page anyway. A keyword-based crit also checks the **presence** of each positive keyword you chose and whether a squint produces the assigned feeling. **Check presence, not just absence** — that is the gap the human reviewer fell into.
- The concrete replacements all live in `references/design-lexicon.md`; pull them per section with `node scripts/keyword-picker.js pick --section <k> --mood <feelings>`.
